About This Toolkit

This self-assessment kit helps UAE businesses evaluate their VAT compliance status and prepare for Federal Tax Authority (FTA) audits. Use this document to:

  • Identify compliance gaps before an FTA audit
  • Ensure your ERP/accounting system is properly configured
  • Verify documentation requirements are met
  • Prepare corrective actions for identified issues
⚠️ Disclaimer: This toolkit provides general guidance only. For specific compliance questions, consult with a registered tax agent.

Section 1: VAT Registration Compliance

  • VAT registration certificate displayed at business premises
  • TRN (Tax Registration Number) printed on all invoices
  • TRN format is correct (15 digits)
  • Business activity matches registration category
  • All branches/locations covered under registration
  • Registration details updated after any business changes
Penalty Risk: Operating without valid registration: AED 20,000 + AED 10,000/month

Section 2: Tax Invoice Compliance

Full Tax Invoice Requirements (B2B over AED 10,000)

  • Words "Tax Invoice" displayed on invoice
  • Unique sequential invoice number
  • Invoice date
  • Supplier name and TRN
  • Customer name and TRN (if registered)
  • Supplier address
  • Customer address
  • Description of goods/services
  • Quantity of goods (if applicable)
  • Unit price (excluding VAT)
  • Discount amount (if applicable)
  • VAT rate applied (5%, 0%, or exempt)
  • VAT amount in AED
  • Total amount payable in AED

Simplified Tax Invoice (B2C or under AED 10,000)

  • Words "Tax Invoice" displayed
  • Supplier name and TRN
  • Invoice date
  • Description of goods/services
  • Total amount inclusive of VAT
Common Errors Found in Audits:
  • Missing customer TRN on B2B invoices
  • VAT amount not in AED
  • Incorrect VAT rate applied
  • Non-sequential invoice numbers

Section 3: VAT Return Filing

  • Returns filed before deadline (28th of following month)
  • All taxable supplies correctly reported
  • Zero-rated supplies properly classified
  • Exempt supplies correctly excluded from VAT calculation
  • Input tax only claimed on eligible expenses
  • Blocked input tax properly excluded (entertainment, vehicles)
  • Import VAT correctly reported (if applicable)
  • Reverse charge VAT applied where required
  • Adjustment for bad debts properly calculated
  • Payment made by deadline
Filing Deadlines:

Section 4: Input Tax Recovery

Eligible for Full Recovery

  • Purchases directly related to taxable supplies
  • Valid tax invoice retained
  • Claimed in correct tax period

Blocked Input Tax (Cannot Recover)

  • Entertainment expenses (unless for employees)
  • Motor vehicles (unless for resale or business use)
  • Personal expenses
  • Purchases for exempt supplies

Partial Recovery (Apportionment Required)

  • Mixed-use expenses apportioned correctly
  • Apportionment method approved by FTA
  • Annual adjustment calculated if required

Section 5: Record Keeping

  • All records retained for minimum 5 years
  • Records stored in UAE or accessible electronically
  • Arabic records available or translatable on request
  • Sales invoices (copies)
  • Purchase invoices (originals)
  • Customs documentation
  • VAT return working papers
  • Bank statements
  • Contracts and agreements
  • Import/export documentation

Section 6: System Configuration Audit

  • ERP/accounting system is FTA-approved (if claiming)
  • VAT rates correctly configured (5%, 0%, exempt)
  • Tax codes assigned to all products/services
  • Invoice templates include all required fields
  • Automatic TRN validation enabled
  • VAT reports can be generated matching FTA format
  • Audit trail enabled for all transactions
  • User access controls configured
